About the Pluto-Charon Image

This NASA image of Pluto and Charon was taken with the Hubble Space Telescope using the European Space Agency's Faint Object Camera or FOC. It is currently one of the best images available and is unlikely to be substantially supplanted until 2015 when the New Horizons probe, launched in January 2006, is expected to fly by Pluto en-route to the Kuiper Belt.

Pluton was the proposed name for trans-Nepturian objects - though geologists have long used this word to mean a body of igneous rock. The notion of a gaggle of small planetoids revolving around a set of central applications seems to fit with the application model described here-in.
